Lapifuse Bunionectomy

Advanced 3D bunion correction that addresses the root cause of bunions for lasting results and a faster return to activity.

What is Lapifuse?

Lapifuse (also known as Lapiplasty) is a revolutionary approach to bunion correction that treats bunions as a three-dimensional problem rather than just a bump on the side of the foot. Traditional bunion surgery often only addresses the visible bump, but Lapifuse corrects the unstable joint at the root of the problem.

This advanced technique realigns the entire metatarsal bone in all three dimensions, then fuses the unstable joint to prevent the bunion from returning. The result is a more anatomically correct correction with lower recurrence rates.

Benefits of Lapifuse

  • 3D Correction: Addresses the bunion in all three planes for comprehensive realignment
  • Lower Recurrence: By fusing the unstable joint, the bunion is less likely to return
  • Faster Weight-Bearing: Many patients can bear weight in a boot within days of surgery
  • Improved Stability: The procedure restores natural foot mechanics
  • Lasting Results: Designed to provide permanent correction

Who is a Candidate?

Lapifuse may be right for you if:

  • You have moderate to severe bunion deformity
  • Conservative treatments have not provided adequate relief
  • You want a correction with lower risk of recurrence
  • You're looking to return to activity as quickly as possible
  • You have hypermobility or instability at the first TMT joint

The Procedure

During the Lapifuse procedure, Dr. Johnson uses specialized instrumentation to rotate and realign the entire metatarsal bone. The unstable joint at the base of the metatarsal is then fused using titanium plates from leading manufacturers such as Arthrex, Sky Surgical, and Enovis, providing permanent stabilization.

The surgery is typically performed on an outpatient basis under anesthesia. Dr. Johnson will discuss your specific case and what to expect during your consultation.

Recovery

One of the advantages of Lapifuse is the recovery timeline:

  • Days 1-2: Rest and elevation with minimal weight-bearing
  • Weeks 1-6: Weight-bearing in a protective boot
  • Weeks 6-10: Transition to supportive shoes
  • 3+ Months: Return to full activities including exercise

Individual recovery times vary based on your health, activity level, and how well you follow post-operative instructions.
