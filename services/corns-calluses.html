Corns & Callus Removal

Don't let painful corns and calluses slow you down. We provide safe, effective treatment to relieve pain and prevent these common foot problems from returning.

What Are Corns and Calluses?

Corns and calluses are areas of thickened, hardened skin that develop as your body's protective response to friction and pressure. While they serve a protective purpose, they can become painful and unsightly when they grow too thick.

Calluses are larger, flatter areas of thick skin that usually form on the soles of your feet, particularly under the heels or balls of the feet. Corns are smaller and deeper, with a hard center surrounded by inflamed skin, typically forming on the tops or sides of toes.

Types of Corns

  • Hard Corns: Small, dense areas typically on the tops of toes or outer edge of the small toe
  • Soft Corns: Whitish, rubbery lesions usually found between toes where moisture accumulates
  • Seed Corns: Tiny corns that form on the bottom of the foot, often in clusters

Common Causes

  • Ill-fitting shoes that are too tight or too loose
  • High heels that increase pressure on the forefoot
  • Not wearing socks or wearing socks that don't fit properly
  • Foot deformities such as bunions, hammertoes, or bone spurs
  • Abnormal gait patterns that create uneven pressure
  • Repetitive activities that cause friction

Professional Treatment

While over-the-counter treatments exist, professional care is the safest and most effective approach, especially if you have diabetes or circulation problems.

  • Professional Debridement: Dr. Johnson carefully removes thickened skin using sterile instruments
  • Padding and Cushioning: Custom pads to redistribute pressure and protect affected areas
  • Custom Orthotics: Address underlying biomechanical issues that cause uneven pressure
  • Footwear Recommendations: Guidance on proper shoe selection
  • Treatment of Underlying Conditions: Addressing bunions, hammertoes, or other issues

Why Avoid DIY Removal

We strongly advise against attempting to cut or shave corns and calluses at home. This can lead to:

  • Infection from non-sterile instruments
  • Cutting too deep and causing bleeding or wounds
  • Making the problem worse by damaging healthy tissue
  • Serious complications for diabetic patients

Over-the-counter corn removal pads containing salicylic acid can also be harmful, particularly for those with diabetes, as they can damage healthy skin and cause ulcers.

Prevention Tips

  • Wear shoes that fit properly with adequate toe room
  • Choose shoes with cushioned soles
  • Wear moisture-wicking socks
  • Keep feet moisturized to prevent dry, cracked skin
  • Use pumice stone gently after bathing to manage calluses
  • Consider custom orthotics if you have foot deformities
  • Address underlying conditions like bunions or hammertoes
